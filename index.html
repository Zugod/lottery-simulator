How hard is it to win the lottery?
Winning the lottery jackpot is far harder than most people imagine. Even if you play regularly for years, your real chance of hitting the jackpot usually stays extremely low.

This simulator helps show the reality of lottery odds by using random simulations and probability-based estimates. Your chosen numbers are not smarter than random numbers, and playing longer mainly increases how much money you spend.

The most useful way to think about lottery odds is not just "1 in X", but:
- how many years you would need on average
- how much money you would spend
- how tiny your lifetime jackpot chance really is
FAQ

Do lucky numbers help?

No. Lucky numbers have exactly the same jackpot chance as random numbers.

Why are the years so high?

Because jackpot odds are extremely small. Even with regular play, the expected wait can be tens of thousands or even millions of years.

Is this a prediction?

No. The simulator does not predict future draws. It shows random simulations and probability-based estimates.

Can a system beat the lottery?

Not in normal jackpot games. Systems may change how you choose numbers, but they do not change the true jackpot odds.
